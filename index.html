01 · The Concept

A wellness "third place," not just a spa

The hook isn't biohacking or luxury pampering — it's belonging. The Bathhouse is built around an authentic Nordic-style heat-and-cold circuit, but its real product is connection: a warm, design-forward room where regulars run into each other, slow down, and feel part of something. Swimwear-on, co-ed, come-and-go.

Why

People are starved for unstructured, screen-free social spaces that aren't a bar. Sauna culture delivers exactly that — and recovery is having a genuine cultural moment.

What

1 communal sauna, 1 steam room, hot/cold/temperate tubs, showers, and a communal lounge serving tea, coffee, kombucha, and light snacks. No alcohol, no gym — yet.

Who

Locals seeking a regular ritual and a community — wellness-curious professionals, couples, and friends, ages ~25–55, in and around downtown Vancouver.

What makes it stand out

  • Community-first. Programming, regulars, and a lounge designed for lingering — not a clinical in-and-out.
  • Authentic thermal circuit. A real hot→cold→rest loop done properly, not an infrared box bolted onto a gym.
  • Approachable, not intimidating. Swimwear-on and co-ed lowers the barrier for first-timers.

The bigger vision (Phase 2+)

This starter location proves the concept and the community. The full vision layers on a gym, more soaking, a full juice bar, and expanded social space — either by expanding here or opening a flagship second site once the model is proven.

We're deliberately starting lean to validate demand and protect your capital.

04 · Membership & Pricing

Two tiers, all-inclusive, built for regulars

Membership is the financial backbone — recurring, predictable revenue and the engine of community. The structure is deliberately simple: one individual membership with two access levels (Anytime and Off-Peak), everything included. No towel fees, no add-on nickel-and-diming — that protects the "third place" feel.

Off-Peak Daytime

  • Same all-inclusive perks: tea, snacks, towel, locker

Why these prices

You sit intentionally below Portland's premium players (Knot Springs $200–400, Othership ~$333) and above infrared-only studios ($40–50). $139 anytime matches mid-market full-circuit comps (ROK $150, Red Rock $125) while the $99 off-peak tier widens the funnel and monetizes dead daytime hours.

07 · Startup Cost Breakdown

The lean-launch plan: opening for ~$250k, self-funded

No financing — so the plan is engineered to open within a ~$250k cash ceiling without it. Three decisions get there: keep the full 2,200 sq ft (protect the community feel), find a wet-ready space to slash buildout, and phase the steam room and temperate tub into Phase 1b. The table below reflects that lean-launch configuration.

Cost to open (lean launch)

Deferred to Phase 1b (funded from cash flow once members ramp): steam room ~$16k, temperate tub ~$9k.

The decisions behind the number

  • Wet-ready space is the big lever. A former restaurant/gym/salon/laundromat with existing plumbing, drains & ventilation drops buildout from ~$65 to ~$45/sq ft — about $44k. Tradeoff: you take what's available, so prioritize bones over address.
  • Phase the equipment. Open with the core circuit (sauna + hot & cold tubs) — a complete contrast experience — and add steam + temperate within year one. Defers ~$25k.
  • Founding-member presale. ~60 members pre-sold before opening seeds cash and shrinks the ramp cushion. Push to 80 to create more margin.
  • Keep ~2,200 sq ft. You chose to protect the lounge and full member ceiling rather than shrink — savings come from sourcing & phasing instead.

A standard (not wet-ready) retail shell would push total cash to launch to ~$280k — over the ceiling. Wet-ready is what makes self-funding work.

From plan to open in 6–12 months

Finalize this model, set the budget ceiling, scout wet-ready spaces, line up financing options, and start a founding-member waitlist to test demand.

Lease, design & approvals

Sign lease, finalize layout, order long-lead equipment, and pull plumbing/ventilation/building permits — the critical-path item for any wet space.

Construction, waterproofing, install the sauna, steam room & tubs, build the lounge, brand the space, hire and train staff.

Months 7–9 · Pre-open
Soft launch

Founding-member previews, refine flow and capacity, dial in operations, convert the waitlist to paying members.

Month 9+ · Open & grow
Public opening

Grand opening, build the membership base toward maturity, deepen the community, and gather data for the Phase 2 expansion case.

09 · Risks & Open Questions

What we still need to pin down

Key risks

  • Capital vs. ambition. Wet buildout likely exceeds the lean budget — needs a financing or scope decision.
  • Utilities & water. Now grounded locally (~$3.2k/mo electric+gas+water), but a wet facility's demand charges and water turnover can surprise — worth real quotes.
  • Capacity math. Open social bathing must be capped for safety/comfort — caps the day-pass ceiling.
  • Permitting timeline. Health/building approvals for wet facilities can slip the opening.

Open questions for our next session

  • Do you have a target space or neighborhood block in mind yet?
  • How will you bridge the ~$340k cash to launch vs. the ~$185k budget — savings, SBA loan, equipment financing, or a partner?
  • Can you run a founding-member presale before opening to seed both cash and demand?
  • Traditional (wood/electric) sauna, infrared, or a mix?
  • What's the off-peak window exactly (which days/hours qualify for the $99 tier)?
